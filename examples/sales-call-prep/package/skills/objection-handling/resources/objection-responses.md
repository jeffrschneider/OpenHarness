# Objection Response Scripts

## Pricing Objections

### "It's too expensive"

**Response Framework:**
1. Acknowledge the concern
2. Understand the comparison point
3. Reframe to value/ROI
4. Offer proof

**Script:**
> "I appreciate you being direct about that. Can I ask—when you say too expensive, what are you comparing it to? [Listen] Got it. Let me share how other [similar companies] have thought about this. [Customer X] initially had the same concern, but when we mapped out [specific value], they actually found [ROI metric]. Would it be helpful if I walked through that analysis for your situation?"

### "We don't have budget"

**Response Options:**

*If timing issue:*
> "Totally understand—budget cycles are real. When does your next planning period start? I'd love to stay in touch and make sure we're on your radar when the time is right."

*If prioritization issue:*
> "Makes sense. Out of curiosity, what did get budget this cycle? [Listen] Interesting—we actually help with [related area]. Would it make sense to explore if there's overlap?"

### "Competitor X is cheaper"

**Response Framework:**
1. Don't bash competitor
2. Understand the comparison
3. Differentiate on value
4. Address TCO

**Script:**
> "Good to know you're looking at options. Are you comparing similar scope? [Listen] That makes sense. A few things our customers have found when comparing: [Differentiator 1], [Differentiator 2]. Also, when you factor in [hidden cost], the total cost picture often looks different. Would it be helpful to do a side-by-side?"

## Timing Objections

### "Not a priority right now"

**Response:**
> "I hear you—there's always a lot competing for attention. What is top priority for you right now? [Listen] That's interesting because [connection to their priority]. Would it make sense to explore how we might actually help with that?"

### "We're mid-contract with [incumbent]"

**Response:**
> "Totally understand—switching costs are real. When does that contract come up? [Listen] Would it make sense to start the conversation a few months before so you have options when the time comes? I can also share what we're seeing other [similar companies] do in the meantime."

### "Call me back in 6 months"

**Response:**
> "Absolutely, I'll do that. Before I go—is there a specific reason for the 6-month timeline? [Listen] Got it. Would it be helpful if I sent you [relevant content] in the meantime so you're up to speed when we reconnect?"

## Authority Objections

### "I need to check with my boss"

**Response:**
> "Of course—these decisions don't happen in a vacuum. What do you think their main questions or concerns will be? [Listen] Would it be helpful if I put together a one-pager addressing those points? Or I'd be happy to join that conversation if that would be useful."

### "This requires a committee decision"

**Response:**
> "Understood—who else would typically be involved in a decision like this? [Listen] What do you think will resonate with each of them? I can help you build the internal case if that would be useful."

---

## Trust/Risk Objections

### "We've never heard of you"

**Response:**
> "That's fair—we're growing fast but not yet a household name. A few companies you might know that use us: [Customer 1], [Customer 2]. I can also connect you with a reference in your industry if that would help."

### "What if it doesn't work?"

**Response:**
> "Great question—and the right one to ask. Here's how we de-risk it: [Pilot program / POC / Guarantee]. We're confident enough in the results that we're willing to [specific commitment]. Does that help address the concern?"

---

## Status Quo Objections

### "We're fine with what we have"

**Response:**
> "That's great to hear things are working. Out of curiosity, if you could wave a magic wand and improve one thing about how you [relevant process] today, what would it be? [Listen] Interesting—that's actually where we see the biggest impact."

### "We tried something similar and it didn't work"

**Response:**
> "I appreciate you sharing that—bad experiences are hard to shake. Can I ask what happened? [Listen] That's frustrating. Here's how we're different: [Specific differentiation]. Would you be open to seeing how we'd approach it differently?"
