# Japan Customs & Etiquette

## Essential Etiquette

### Greetings
- Bow when greeting (slight nod is fine for tourists)
- Business cards exchanged with both hands, study it before putting away
- Remove shoes when entering homes, temples, some restaurants

### Dining
- Say "itadakimasu" before eating, "gochisousama" after
- Don't stick chopsticks upright in rice (funeral ritual)
- Slurping noodles is polite - shows enjoyment
- Don't tip - it can be insulting

### Public Behavior
- Keep voice low on trains
- Don't eat while walking
- Stand on correct side of escalators (left in Tokyo, right in Osaka)
- No phone calls on trains

### Temples & Shrines
- Wash hands and mouth at temizuya (water pavilion)
- Shrine: clap twice, bow twice, clap once
- Temple: just bow, no clapping
- Don't photograph monks without permission

## Money & Payments

- Cash is still king - many places don't accept cards
- 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards
- No tipping anywhere
- Convenience stores (konbini) are essential - open 24/7

## Transportation

- Trains run exactly on time
- Get IC card (Suica/Pasmo) immediately
- Last trains around midnight - plan accordingly
- Taxis expensive but useful late night

## Useful Phrases

| Japanese | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| Sumimasen | Excuse me / Sorry |
| Arigatou gozaimasu | Thank you (polite) |
| Eigo wo hanasemasu ka? | Do you speak English? |
| Ikura desu ka? | How much? |
| Oishii! | Delicious! |
