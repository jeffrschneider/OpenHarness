# Japan Cultural Customs & Etiquette

## General Etiquette

### Greetings
- Bow when greeting (slight nod is fine for tourists)
- Business cards: Present and receive with both hands, study before putting away
- "Sumimasen" (excuse me) is very useful

### Public Behavior
- Keep voice low on public transit (trains are silent)
- No eating while walking (except at festivals)
- Line up orderly for everything
- Stand on the correct side of escalators (varies by city)

### Tipping
- **Do not tip** - It's not customary and can be seen as rude
- Exceptional service is expected as standard
- Some tourist areas now accept tips from foreigners

## Dining Etiquette

### Before Eating
- Say "itadakimasu" (I humbly receive)
- Hot towel (oshibori) is for hands only, not face

### Chopsticks
- Never stick chopsticks upright in rice (funeral ritual)
- Don't pass food chopstick-to-chopstick
- Don't point with chopsticks
- Rest on chopstick holder when not using

### Drinking
- Don't pour your own drink; pour for others
- Wait for "kanpai" (cheers) before drinking
- Slurping noodles is acceptable and shows appreciation

### After Eating
- Say "gochisousama deshita" (thank you for the meal)
- Don't leave large amounts of food uneaten

## Visiting Temples & Shrines

### Shrines (Shinto)
1. Bow slightly at the torii gate
2. Walk to the side (center is for gods)
3. Purify at the water basin (temizu)
4. At the altar: bow twice, clap twice, bow once

### Temples (Buddhist)
- Remove shoes where indicated
- Photography may be restricted inside
- Bow once at the altar
- Incense smoke can be wafted for blessing

## Onsen (Hot Springs) & Baths

### Rules
- Wash thoroughly before entering the bath
- No swimsuits (nude bathing)
- No towels in the water (place on head)
- Tattoos: Some onsen prohibit; check beforehand
- No photos

### Process
1. Remove all clothes in changing area
2. Wash at shower station (seated)
3. Rinse completely
4. Enter bath slowly
5. Soak quietly

## Shoes

### Remove shoes at:
- All homes
- Traditional restaurants (tatami floors)
- Temple interiors
- Some museums and attractions

### Slippers
- Use provided slippers indoors
- Special toilet slippers for bathroom (don't forget to change!)
- Never wear slippers on tatami

## Business Situations

- Punctuality is critical
- Exchange business cards at start of meeting
- Decisions take time; don't rush
- "Yes" doesn't always mean agreement
