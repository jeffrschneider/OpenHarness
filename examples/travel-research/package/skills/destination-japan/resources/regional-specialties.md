# Japan Regional Food Specialties

## Tokyo

- **Monjayaki** - Runny savory pancake (Tsukishima area)
- **Edomae Sushi** - Original Tokyo-style sushi
- **Ramen** - Many styles available, but Tokyo-style is shoyu (soy sauce) based
- **Chankonabe** - Sumo wrestler hot pot
- **Fukagawa-meshi** - Clam rice bowl

## Kyoto

- **Kaiseki** - Traditional multi-course haute cuisine
- **Yudofu** - Simple tofu hot pot
- **Matcha** - Green tea everything (sweets, ice cream, noodles)
- **Kyo-yasai** - Kyoto vegetables
- **Yuba** - Tofu skin
- **Saba-zushi** - Pressed mackerel sushi

## Osaka

- **Takoyaki** - Octopus balls (must-try street food)
- **Okonomiyaki** - Savory pancake (Osaka-style)
- **Kushikatsu** - Deep-fried skewers (no double dipping!)
- **Kitsune Udon** - Udon with sweet fried tofu
- **Tecchiri** - Pufferfish hot pot

## Hiroshima

- **Hiroshima-style Okonomiyaki** - Layered, with noodles
- **Momiji Manju** - Maple leaf-shaped cakes
- **Oysters** - Miyajima is famous for grilled oysters
- **Anago** - Conger eel (different from unagi)

## Fukuoka / Hakata

- **Hakata Ramen** - Rich, creamy tonkotsu (pork bone) broth
- **Mentaiko** - Spicy cod roe
- **Motsunabe** - Offal hot pot
- **Yatai** - Street food stalls along the river

## Hokkaido

- **Miso Ramen** - Sapporo specialty
- **Soup Curry** - Sapporo-originated curry style
- **Jingisukan** - Grilled lamb BBQ
- **Seafood** - Crab, sea urchin, scallops
- **Dairy** - Famous for ice cream and cheese
- **Shiroi Koibito** - White chocolate cookies

## Okinawa

- **Goya Champuru** - Bitter melon stir-fry
- **Soki Soba** - Pork rib noodle soup
- **Taco Rice** - Tex-Mex meets Japan
- **Sata Andagi** - Okinawan donuts
- **Awamori** - Local rice spirit

## Nagoya

- **Miso Katsu** - Tonkatsu with red miso sauce
- **Hitsumabushi** - Grilled eel rice with variations
- **Tebasaki** - Fried chicken wings
- **Kishimen** - Flat udon noodles

## Kanazawa

- **Jibuni** - Duck and wheat gluten stew
- **Kaga Vegetables** - Local traditional vegetables
- **Gold Leaf** - Used on sweets and even ice cream
- **Fresh Seafood** - Excellent sushi from Sea of Japan

## Nara

- **Kakinoha-zushi** - Sushi wrapped in persimmon leaves
- **Narazuke** - Vegetables pickled in sake lees
- **Miwa Somen** - Thin wheat noodles

## Tips for Food Travelers

1. **Depachika** - Department store basements have amazing food halls
2. **Konbini** - Convenience store food is surprisingly good
3. **Lunch specials** - High-end restaurants often have affordable lunch sets
4. **Food floors** - Train station buildings have restaurant floors
5. **Seasonal items** - Limited-time regional flavors are popular
