# Age-Appropriate Activities Guide

## Toddlers (1-3 years)

### Best Activities
- Parks and playgrounds
- Aquariums (visual stimulation)
- Short boat rides
- Animal encounters (petting zoos)
- Beach/sand play
- Simple museums with interactive exhibits

### Considerations
- Nap schedule is critical
- Need stroller accessibility
- Short attention spans (30-45 min max)
- Familiar snacks available
- Changing facilities needed

### Skip
- Long museum visits
- Performances requiring silence
- Extensive walking tours
- Anything with long queues

## Preschool (4-5 years)

### Best Activities
- Interactive museums (children's museums, science centers)
- Zoos and aquariums
- Theme parks with kid sections
- Easy hikes (1-2 miles max)
- Cooking classes (simple)
- Character experiences
- Train rides

### Considerations
- Can handle more walking but still tire easily
- Need regular snack/rest breaks
- Interested in "helping" and participating
- Potty breaks needed frequently
- May still need afternoon rest

### Skip
- Art museums (unless specifically interactive)
- Historical tours
- Multi-hour activities without breaks

## Early Elementary (6-8 years)

### Best Activities
- Theme parks
- Interactive museums
- Beginner sports (kayaking, skiing lessons)
- Scavenger hunts
- Nature programs
- Bike tours (flat, short)
- Cooking classes
- Escape rooms (family/kids versions)

### Considerations
- Can read and follow simple instructions
- Interested in learning new skills
- Can handle 4-6 hours of activity with breaks
- Still need frequent food/bathroom breaks
- Enjoy collecting (souvenirs, stamps)

### Best For
- Hands-on experiences
- Activities with clear "achievements"
- Learning something new

## Upper Elementary (9-12 years)

### Best Activities
- Adventure activities (zip-lining, snorkeling)
- Sports and outdoor activities
- Behind-the-scenes tours
- Historical sites with stories
- Technology/gaming experiences
- Escape rooms
- Workshops (art, cooking, crafts)
- Wildlife experiences

### Considerations
- Can handle full-day activities
- Interested in "adult" experiences
- May want some independence
- Peer influence matters
- Can appreciate history with good storytelling

### Best For
- Challenge and achievement
- Learning about different cultures
- Activities they can share with friends later

## Teens (13-17 years)

### Best Activities
- Extreme sports (with training)
- Cultural immersion experiences
- Food tours
- Photography walks
- Adventure activities
- Music/entertainment venues
- Shopping districts
- Night activities (appropriate)

### Considerations
- Include them in planning
- Allow some independent time
- WiFi availability matters
- Later wake-up times preferred
- May want to split from parents occasionally

### Best For
- Experiences they can post about
- Activities matching their interests
- Learning practical skills
- Meeting other teens (tours, classes)

## Multi-Age Family Tips

### Balancing Different Ages
1. **Alternate**: Kid activity, then teen/adult activity
2. **Split up**: Parents divide with different kids
3. **Find crossovers**: Activities all ages enjoy
4. **Downtime**: Younger kids rest while older explore

### Universal Crowd-Pleasers
- Beach/pool time
- Cooking classes
- Animal experiences
- Scenic transportation (boats, trams)
- Ice cream/food tours
- Interactive museums
- Nature experiences

### Strategies
- Morning: Energy-intensive activities
- Midday: Lunch + rest/pool time
- Afternoon: Lighter activities
- Evening: Relaxed dinner + early bedtime for little ones
